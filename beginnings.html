His Beginnings

  • Ajaccio 1769

    Napoleone Buonaparte was born, luckily, on the 15th of August 1769 in Ajaccio on the small island of Corsica in the Mediterranean. Why luckily? Because the island had changed masters just the year before. After belonging to the Republic of Genoa, it was now France's. Had this not happened, who knows where fate would have guided the young Corsican? Surely, no one could have expected what heights this young man of Italian blood born in a modest noble family would reach.

  • Military school

    At the age of 9, due to his father's success in securing a royal grant to pay for his scholarship, the young Napoleon is sent on the mainland to commence his education. At school, he would experience mockery from his classmates regarding his Corsican accent and would often get into fights because of it. These experiences did not impede the young Napoleon from performing very well in class, especially in geography and mathematics. He was a very enthusiastic reader and could often be found reading about the lives of the great conquerors of history such as Alexander the Great, Julius Caesar or Frederick II.

  • Toulon 1793

    Bonaparte's first military feat can be traced all the way back to Toulon in the second half of 1793. The British and Spanish had taken control of the city and it was crucial that the French dislodge them from this strategic point from which their enemies could prepare and launch dangerous attacks. Although he didn't command the whole operation, the taking of Fort de l'Eguillette, an advantageous position from which the French artillery could bombard the British and Spanish ships, was his idea and the brilliance of it was immediately recognized by his superiors.

  • General of the Army of Italy

    After having saved the Republic from a royalist insurrection in Paris in September 1795, Napoleon Bonaparte was given the command of the Army of Italy in early 1796. It was both a great honour and a great responsabilty for someone who was still so young. Remember he was only 26 years old. It was hard to win over the hearts of the French soldiers who had been fighting for years already, but he managed it, with both his grandiose speeches and brave actions. After a magnificent two year campaign, he even succeeded in bringing down the hereditary rival of France: Austria.
