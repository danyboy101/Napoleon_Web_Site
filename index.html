Who was he?

Was he a monstrous tyrant whose ambition and lust for power pushed him to go to war with all of Europe, bringing fear and death to the continent? Or was he rather the greatest hero of France who finally brought stabilty and prosperity to the nation after a decade of turbulence caused by the Revolution? To most people Napoleon Bonaparte is somewhere in the middle. Whatever one might think about him, however, it is undeniable that few men have had such a lasting impact on the world and the story of his life, of his rise to power from humble origins, of his wars against all of Europe can leave nobody cold and indifferent. He is mostly known for his military prowess and while he is undoubtedly the greatest military commander to have ever lived, there is much more to him than that. His iconic hat and posture with the hand hidden in the jacket is recognized internationally. One could discuss for hours his political visions, his ideas about society and the Revolution, all the different ways his defeats could have gone differently, his talent in enflamming the passion of his men or even his great eye for art and the grandiose. We hope to present here a taste of who Napoleon Bonaparte was and what he did after which you will hopefully have a new-found interest in the life of the Emperor of the French and learn more about his incredible life.
