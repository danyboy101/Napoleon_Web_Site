His End

  • Battle of Waterloo (June 18th 1815)

    His last battle. The last after a long series of glorious victories. It was also his last chance to establish himself and his dynasty as permanent rulers of France. In the evening of the 18th of June 1815, it seemed that the Emperor and his army had won the battle against the Duke of Wellington, up until Bl√ºcher and his Prussians arrived at the last second and caused the French to flee. Waterloo might still be a name which inspires much nostalgia to French hearts. Many poems, songs and books have been written about it in the nineteenth century. The memory of Waterloo is therefore hard to forget and the limits of 1815 are still the ones marking off the northern borders of France to this day.

  • Exile on Saint-Helena

    After waterloo, Napoleon surrendered himself to the British, thinking this would lead to an honourable end to his story. It wasn't exactly the case. The British authorities decided to exile him on the island of Saint Helena in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean where British troops were always present to keep an eye on him and prevent any attempt to escape. There Napoleon started a new kind of battle: the battle of the mind. His work was focused on rewriting his story to rehabilitate himself in the eyes of his countrymen and to pave the way for a new Bonaparte to sit on the throne of France one day...
